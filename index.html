Part 1 — Chains

Decoding America's Largest Restaurant Empires

By engineering growth metrics and aggregating segment performance, we uncovered which dining categories dominate — and which are poised to surge.

Finding 01 · Segment Analysis

Quick Service chains outpace all other categories by a wide margin

After aggregating mean sales across all 20+ segment categories, Quick Service & Burger, Quick Service & Mexican, and Quick Service & Coffee Cafe claimed the top three spots — each averaging over $3 billion in annual sales. The gap between Quick Service and non-Quick Service restaurants was quantified at over $1,700M in average sales.

Quick Service avg sales premium: $1,700M+ over non-QS chains

Finding 02 · Growth Classification

Only ~9% of top chains achieved "Rapid Growth" in the pandemic year

A custom 5-tier growth classification system was engineered using year-over-year sales data — converting percentage strings to numerical proportions and applying categorical thresholds. Category 5 (rapid increase, ≥10% YOY) captured just ~9% of chains, led by chicken and delivery-friendly concepts. Reflecting COVID-19's impact, Growth Category 1 (rapid decline) was the most common — capturing 60% of chains.

~60% of chains saw rapid decline (Cat 1) · only ~9% achieved rapid growth (Cat 5)

Finding 03 · Investment Targets

Five segment categories lead on growth, with three clear frontrunners for investment

Ranking all 20 segment categories by mean growth category score revealed five top-performing segments, charted below. The top three demonstrated the strongest momentum heading into the post-pandemic recovery, making them the most attractive for capital allocation.

#1 QS & Chicken · #2 QS & Pizza · #3 QS & Mexican

Finding 04 · Top Segments by Count

Casual Dining dominates by count, but loses to QS on revenue

Filtering and categorizing the Top 250 dataset revealed that "Casual Dining & Varied Menu" is the single most common segment category. However, when normalized by average sales, Quick Service categories dominate — illustrating that volume of chains doesn't equal volume of revenue.

Casual Dining: most chains · Quick Service: most revenue

Finding · State Analysis

State-level proportion of 4-star+ independent restaurants

By merging Yelp ratings with sales data and grouping by state, we computed the proportion of top independent restaurants with at least 4 stars per state across 20 states. This revealed significant geographic variation — Texas had the lowest proportion at just 33%, while D.C., Louisiana, Indiana, and Virginia each achieved a perfect 100%.

Texas: 33% · New York: 60% · D.C. / Louisiana / Indiana / Virginia: 100%
